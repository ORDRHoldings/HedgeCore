## MASTER SUBSCRIPTION AGREEMENT

**This Master Subscription Agreement** (the "**Agreement**") is entered into as of the date last signed below (the "**Effective Date**") by and between **[ORDR TreasuryFX legal entity name]**, a [jurisdiction] [entity type] with its principal place of business at [address] ("**ORDR**"), and the customer identified on the applicable Order Form ("**Customer**").

ORDR and Customer are each a "**Party**" and together the "**Parties**."

### 1. Definitions

**1.1** "**Order Form**" means an ordering document or online order specifying the Services to be provided by ORDR to Customer that has been signed or accepted by both Parties.

**1.2** "**Services**" means the ORDR TreasuryFX cloud-based hedge calculation, governance, and reporting platform identified in the applicable Order Form, together with associated software, documentation, updates, and support.

**1.3** "**Customer Data**" means electronic data and information submitted by or for Customer to the Services.

**1.4** "**Documentation**" means the user guides, technical documentation, and similar materials made available by ORDR.

**1.5** "**Affiliate**" means any entity that directly or indirectly controls, is controlled by, or is under common control with the subject entity.

**1.6** "**Confidential Information**" has the meaning set forth in Section 8.

**1.7** "**Subscription Term**" means the period during which Customer is authorized to use the Services as set forth in the applicable Order Form.

### 2. Services and Access

**2.1 Provision of Services.** ORDR will (a) make the Services available to Customer pursuant to this Agreement, the applicable Order Form, and the Documentation; (b) provide standard support for the Services at no additional charge; and (c) use commercially reasonable efforts to make the Services available 24 hours a day, 7 days a week, except for (i) planned downtime (of which ORDR shall give advance notice via the Services), and (ii) any unavailability caused by circumstances beyond ORDR's reasonable control.

**2.2 User Accounts.** Customer is responsible for the activities of all users of its accounts. Customer will (a) ensure all users keep their credentials confidential; (b) notify ORDR promptly of any unauthorized use; and (c) comply with the Acceptable Use Policy referenced in the Order Form.

**2.3 Service Levels.** If specified in the Order Form, ORDR will provide the Services in accordance with the Service Level Agreement (the "**SLA**") attached as Exhibit A. Customer's exclusive remedy for any failure to meet the SLA is the service credit set forth in the SLA.

**2.4 Modifications.** ORDR may modify the Services from time to time, provided that such modifications do not materially diminish the functionality of the Services in effect on the Effective Date.

### 3. Fees and Payment

**3.1 Fees.** Customer will pay all fees specified in the applicable Order Form. All fees are non-refundable except as expressly set forth in this Agreement.

**3.2 Invoicing.** Unless otherwise specified in the Order Form, ORDR will invoice Customer annually in advance. Invoiced amounts are due within thirty (30) days from the invoice date. Customer is responsible for providing complete and accurate billing information.

**3.3 Late Payments.** Any undisputed amount not paid when due will accrue interest at the lesser of 1.5% per month or the maximum rate permitted by law. ORDR may suspend the Services upon thirty (30) days written notice for any undisputed amount not paid.

**3.4 Taxes.** Fees do not include taxes. Customer is responsible for all sales, use, value-added, and similar taxes, except for taxes based on ORDR's net income.

**3.5 Auto-Renewal.** Each Order Form will automatically renew for additional terms equal to the original Subscription Term unless either Party gives written notice of non-renewal at least sixty (60) days before the end of the then-current term. ORDR may increase fees on renewal upon at least sixty (60) days advance written notice; renewal fee increases shall not exceed [REVIEW: 7%] year-over-year except for changes in tier or scope.

### 4. Customer Data and Security

**4.1 Customer Data Ownership.** As between the Parties, Customer retains all right, title, and interest in and to Customer Data. Customer grants ORDR a non-exclusive, worldwide, royalty-free license to use, copy, store, transmit, modify, create derivative works of, and display the Customer Data solely as necessary to provide the Services.

**4.2 Security.** ORDR will maintain administrative, physical, and technical safeguards designed to protect the security, confidentiality, and integrity of Customer Data, as further described in the Security Documentation made available to Customer.

**4.3 Data Processing Addendum.** Where ORDR processes Personal Data (as defined in applicable data protection laws) on behalf of Customer, the Parties' Data Processing Addendum (the "**DPA**") is incorporated by reference and forms part of this Agreement.

**4.4 Customer Data Export.** During the Subscription Term and for thirty (30) days after termination, Customer may export Customer Data in machine-readable format using the export functionality made available within the Services.

**4.5 Data Deletion.** Within thirty (30) days following termination, ORDR will delete Customer Data, except (a) Customer Data on the WORM audit ledger and regulatory submission records, which ORDR retains as required by applicable financial-services and accounting laws and (b) backup copies, which will be deleted in the ordinary course of ORDR's backup retention cycle (no longer than ninety (90) days).

### 5. Customer Responsibilities

**5.1** Customer is responsible for (a) all activity occurring under its accounts; (b) compliance with all applicable laws and regulations in its use of the Services; (c) the accuracy, quality, and legality of Customer Data; (d) maintaining the confidentiality of authentication credentials; and (e) using the Services only in accordance with the Documentation and the Acceptable Use Policy.

**5.2 Restrictions.** Customer will not (and will not permit any third party to): (a) reverse engineer, decompile, or disassemble the Services, except to the extent applicable law expressly permits; (b) use the Services to develop a competing product; (c) use the Services in violation of any applicable law; (d) circumvent any access or use restrictions; (e) use the Services to store or transmit malicious code; or (f) interfere with or disrupt the integrity or performance of the Services.

**6.3 Aggregated Data.** ORDR may collect, use, and disclose aggregated and anonymized data derived from Customer's use of the Services for any business purpose, provided such data does not identify Customer or any individual.

### 7. Term and Termination

**7.1 Term.** This Agreement begins on the Effective Date and continues until all Order Forms have expired or been terminated.

**7.2 Termination for Cause.** Either Party may terminate this Agreement (or any Order Form) immediately upon written notice if the other Party (a) materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure the breach within thirty (30) days after receiving written notice of the breach; or (b) becomes insolvent, files for bankruptcy, or makes an assignment for the benefit of creditors.

**7.3 Effect of Termination.** Upon termination: (a) Customer's right to access and use the Services terminates; (b) ORDR will issue a refund of any prepaid fees covering the period after the effective date of termination if termination is by Customer for ORDR's uncured material breach; (c) Customer Data will be handled as described in Section 4.5.

**7.4 Survival.** Sections 1, 3 (for amounts owed), 4.1, 4.5, 6, 7.4, 8, 9, 10, 11, 12, and any other provisions intended by their nature to survive will survive any termination of this Agreement.

### 8. Confidentiality

**8.1 Definition.** "**Confidential Information**" means non-public information disclosed by one Party to the other that is identified as confidential at the time of disclosure or that should reasonably be understood to be confidential given the nature of the information.

**8.2 Obligations.** The receiving Party will (a) protect the Confidential Information using the same degree of care it uses to protect its own confidential information of like kind, but no less than reasonable care; (b) use Confidential Information only as necessary to perform under this Agreement; and (c) limit access to Confidential Information to those of its personnel who need to know it for the performance of this Agreement and who are bound by confidentiality obligations no less protective.

**8.3 Exclusions.** Confidential Information does not include information that (a) is or becomes publicly available through no fault of the receiving Party; (b) was known to the receiving Party before receipt; (c) is rightfully obtained from a third party without confidentiality obligations; or (d) is independently developed without use of the disclosing Party's Confidential Information.

**8.4 Compelled Disclosure.** A Party may disclose Confidential Information to the extent required by law or court order, provided it gives prompt notice to the disclosing Party (where legally permitted) and reasonable cooperation in seeking a protective order.

## Exhibit A — Service Level Agreement (SLA)

**A.1 Uptime Commitment.** ORDR will use commercially reasonable efforts to maintain Service Availability of at least 99.9% during each calendar month for Customers on the Enterprise tier, and 99.5% for Customers on the Professional tier. Starter tier Services are provided on a best-efforts basis without an Availability commitment.

**A.2 Definitions.** "**Service Availability**" means the percentage of time during a calendar month that the Services are available, calculated as: ((Total minutes in month – Unscheduled Downtime minutes) / Total minutes in month) × 100%.

"**Unscheduled Downtime**" excludes (a) scheduled maintenance windows of which ORDR provides at least 48 hours advance notice; (b) emergency maintenance for security or critical bug fixes; (c) downtime caused by Customer's actions or systems; (d) downtime caused by force majeure events; and (e) downtime of third-party services not under ORDR's direct control (e.g., ERP providers).

**A.3 Service Credits.**

| Monthly Availability | Service Credit (Enterprise) | Service Credit (Professional) |
|---|---|---|
| < 99.9% but ≥ 99.0% | 5% of monthly fees | — |
| < 99.5% but ≥ 99.0% | — | 5% of monthly fees |
| < 99.0% but ≥ 95.0% | 10% of monthly fees | 10% of monthly fees |
| < 95.0% | 25% of monthly fees | 25% of monthly fees |

**A.4 Credit Process.** Customer must request service credits in writing within thirty (30) days after the end of the affected month. Service credits are applied to future invoices and are not paid in cash.

**A.5 Sole Remedy.** Service credits are Customer's exclusive remedy for any failure to meet the Availability commitment.

**A.6 Support.**

| Tier | Channel | Response Time (Severity 1) | Response Time (Severity 2/3) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Starter | Email | 24 business hours | 48 business hours |
| Professional | Email + chat | 8 business hours | 24 business hours |
| Enterprise | Email + chat + phone | 4 hours, 24/7 | 8 business hours |

"**Severity 1**" = Services unavailable or critical functionality unavailable. "**Severity 2**" = significant functionality impaired but workaround exists. "**Severity 3**" = minor issue, no business impact.

## Drafter's notes (delete before sending to counsel)

1. **Liability cap of 12 months fees** is standard SaaS practice; large enterprises sometimes push for 24 months — that's a known concession point.
2. **Auto-renewal at 7%** matches the Common Paper default; some procurement teams demand cap at CPI — be ready to concede CPI + 3%.
3. **Source code escrow** is not in the base agreement — add as an Enterprise rider only, and only for customers paying $144k+/yr.
4. **MFN (most-favored-nation) clauses** — never agree, even when asked. Reject in negotiation.
5. **"Right to audit ORDR's controls"** — concede only with: (a) annual cap on frequency, (b) 30-day notice, (c) Customer pays its own costs, (d) under NDA, (e) limited to security controls (not financials).
6. **Indemnity for IP infringement** — match competitor scope (Kyriba/Trovata standard); don't volunteer broader scope without negotiation.
7. **Data retention on WORM tables** — explicitly carve out from deletion obligations because regulatory retention rules force this.
8. **Governing law** — most US enterprise customers expect Delaware; UK customers expect England & Wales; EU customers will push for their local law. Anchor at your registration; concede to neutral (NY, London, Frankfurt) if it's a top-10 deal.
9. **Insurance requirements** — not in the base agreement; add as Order Form rider for enterprise customers requesting it. Standard: $1M cyber, $1M E&O, $1M general liability.
10. **Section 11.1 cap** — when a customer demands "uncapped liability for security breaches," counter with: cap at 24 months fees + a separate insurance policy covering the gap. Never agree to true uncapped.
