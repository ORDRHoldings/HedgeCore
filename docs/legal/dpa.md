## DATA PROCESSING ADDENDUM

This Data Processing Addendum ("**DPA**") is entered into by and between **ORDR TreasuryFX** ("**Processor**" or "**ORDR**") and the customer identified in the applicable Order Form ("**Controller**" or "**Customer**") and forms part of the Master Subscription Agreement between them (the "**Agreement**").

This DPA governs the Processing of Personal Data by Processor on behalf of Controller in connection with the provision of the Services.

---

### 1. Definitions

Capitalized terms not defined herein have the meaning given in the Agreement or in applicable Data Protection Laws.

**1.1** "**Data Protection Laws**" means: (a) the EU General Data Protection Regulation 2016/679 ("**EU GDPR**"); (b) the UK General Data Protection Regulation as it forms part of the law of England and Wales, Scotland, and Northern Ireland by virtue of section 3 of the European Union (Withdrawal) Act 2018 and as amended by the Data Protection, Privacy and Electronic Communications (Amendments etc.) (EU Exit) Regulations 2019 ("**UK GDPR**"); (c) the Swiss Federal Act on Data Protection ("**FADP**"); (d) the California Consumer Privacy Act, as amended ("**CCPA/CPRA**"); and (e) other applicable privacy and data protection laws.

**1.2** "**Personal Data**", "**Controller**", "**Processor**", "**Sub-processor**", "**Data Subject**", "**Processing**", "**Personal Data Breach**", and "**Special Categories of Personal Data**" have the meanings given to them in the EU GDPR (and equivalent terms under UK GDPR, FADP, and CCPA/CPRA shall be deemed to apply correspondingly).

**1.3** "**SCCs**" means the Standard Contractual Clauses approved by the European Commission in Decision 2021/914 of 4 June 2021, as amended.

**1.4** "**UK Addendum**" means the International Data Transfer Addendum to the EU Commission Standard Contractual Clauses issued by the UK Information Commissioner's Office under section 119A of the UK Data Protection Act 2018 (Version B1.0 in force 21 March 2022 or successor).

**1.5** "**Restricted Transfer**" means a transfer of Personal Data from a Controller in the European Economic Area, the United Kingdom, or Switzerland to a Processor located outside such jurisdiction in a country not covered by an adequacy decision.

---

### 2. Subject Matter, Duration, Nature, and Purpose

**2.1 Roles.** The Parties acknowledge that for the Processing of Personal Data under the Agreement: (a) Customer is the Controller; (b) ORDR is the Processor; (c) ORDR will Process Personal Data on behalf of Customer in accordance with Customer's documented instructions.

**2.2 Subject matter and duration.** The subject matter of Processing is the provision of the Services described in the Agreement. Processing continues for the term of the Agreement and any post-termination period during which ORDR retains Customer Data per the Agreement.

**2.3 Nature and purpose.** The nature and purpose of Processing is to enable Customer to use the Services (FX hedging, governance, hedge accounting, regulatory submissions, audit trail).

**2.4 Categories of Data Subjects and Personal Data.** Set forth in **Annex I**.

---

### 3. Customer Instructions

**3.1** ORDR will Process Personal Data only on documented instructions from Customer, including with regard to transfers of Personal Data to a third country, unless required to do so by Union or Member State law to which ORDR is subject (in which case, ORDR will inform Customer of that legal requirement before Processing, unless that law prohibits such information on important grounds of public interest).

**3.2** The Agreement (including this DPA), the Order Form, the Documentation, and any subsequent written instructions agreed between the Parties constitute Customer's complete and final documented instructions to ORDR for the Processing of Personal Data. Any additional instructions require prior agreement between the Parties (which may include adjustments to fees).

**3.3** ORDR will inform Customer if, in its opinion, an instruction infringes Data Protection Laws.

---

### 4. Confidentiality and Personnel

ORDR will ensure that personnel authorized to Process Personal Data have committed themselves to confidentiality (or are under a statutory obligation of confidentiality) and have received appropriate training on Personal Data protection.

---

### 5. Security

**5.1** Taking into account the state of the art, the costs of implementation, and the nature, scope, context, and purposes of Processing, ORDR will implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to ensure a level of security appropriate to the risk, as further described in **Annex II**.

**5.2** Customer acknowledges and agrees that the security measures in Annex II provide a level of security appropriate to the risk for the categories of Personal Data Processed under the Agreement.

---

### 6. Sub-processors

**6.1 General authorisation.** Customer provides general authorisation for ORDR's engagement of Sub-processors as listed at the URL set forth in **Annex III** (the "**Sub-processor List**").

**6.2 Notification of changes.** ORDR will notify Customer of any intended additions or replacements of Sub-processors at least thirty (30) days in advance, by updating the Sub-processor List and providing email notification to Customer's data protection contact.

**6.3 Right to object.** Customer may object to a new Sub-processor in writing within thirty (30) days of notification on reasonable grounds related to data protection. If Customer objects, the Parties will work in good faith to resolve the objection. If no resolution is reached, Customer may, as its sole remedy, terminate the affected Order Form upon thirty (30) days' written notice and receive a pro-rated refund of prepaid fees for the unused portion of the Subscription Term.

**6.4 Sub-processor obligations.** ORDR will impose data protection obligations on Sub-processors that are no less protective than those set forth in this DPA. ORDR remains liable to Customer for the performance of each Sub-processor's obligations under this DPA.

---

### 7. Data Subject Requests

**7.1** Taking into account the nature of the Processing, ORDR will assist Customer by appropriate technical and organisational measures, insofar as possible, in fulfilling Customer's obligation to respond to requests from Data Subjects exercising their rights under Data Protection Laws.

**7.2** If ORDR receives a request directly from a Data Subject, ORDR will (unless prohibited by law) promptly forward the request to Customer and will not respond to the Data Subject directly other than to acknowledge receipt and direct the Data Subject to Customer.

---

### 8. Personal Data Breach

**8.1** ORDR will notify Customer without undue delay (and in any case within seventy-two (72) hours) after becoming aware of a Personal Data Breach affecting Customer's Personal Data.

**8.2** The notification will include, to the extent known: (a) a description of the nature of the breach; (b) the categories and approximate number of Data Subjects and records concerned; (c) the likely consequences; and (d) the measures taken or proposed to address the breach and mitigate its effects.

**8.3** ORDR will reasonably cooperate with Customer in Customer's investigation, mitigation, and notification of the Personal Data Breach.

---

### 9. Data Protection Impact Assessments and Prior Consultation

ORDR will, taking into account the nature of the Processing and the information available to it, provide Customer with reasonable assistance for the performance of data protection impact assessments and consultations with supervisory authorities under Data Protection Laws.

---

### 10. Audit

**10.1** ORDR will make available to Customer all information reasonably necessary to demonstrate compliance with this DPA, including by providing copies of (a) ORDR's most recent SOC 2 (Type I or Type II, as available) report or equivalent; and (b) the latest summary of penetration testing and security assessments.

**10.2** Customer may, on no more than an annual basis (and in any case upon a Personal Data Breach affecting Customer Personal Data), request additional audit information through a written request submitted at least thirty (30) days in advance. Such audits will be conducted at Customer's expense, by an independent auditor mutually agreed upon, under appropriate confidentiality obligations, and in a manner that does not unreasonably interfere with ORDR's operations.

---

### 11. Return or Deletion of Personal Data

**11.1** Upon termination of the Agreement, Customer may request, within thirty (30) days, that ORDR (a) return Personal Data to Customer in a structured, machine-readable format; or (b) delete Personal Data.

**11.2** Following the thirty (30) day period or earlier upon Customer's written request, ORDR will delete Personal Data, except for Personal Data: (a) on the WORM audit ledger and regulatory submission records, which ORDR retains as required by applicable financial-services and accounting laws; or (b) in backup copies, which will be deleted in the ordinary course of ORDR's backup retention cycle (no longer than ninety (90) days).

**11.3** ORDR will certify deletion in writing upon Customer's reasonable request.

### 13. CCPA Specific Terms

**13.1** Where ORDR processes Personal Information (as defined in CCPA/CPRA) of California consumers on behalf of Customer, ORDR is a Service Provider as defined in CCPA/CPRA. ORDR will not: (a) sell or share the Personal Information; (b) retain, use, or disclose the Personal Information for any purpose other than for the specific purpose of performing the Services; (c) retain, use, or disclose the Personal Information outside the direct business relationship with Customer; or (d) combine the Personal Information with personal information that ORDR receives from another source, except as permitted by CCPA/CPRA.

**13.2** ORDR certifies that it understands the restrictions in Section 13.1 and will comply with them.

---

### 14. General

**14.1 Conflict.** In the event of a conflict between this DPA and the Agreement, this DPA prevails. In the event of a conflict between this DPA and the SCCs / UK Addendum, the SCCs / UK Addendum prevails with respect to the Restricted Transfer.

**14.2 Term.** This DPA is effective from the Effective Date of the Agreement and remains in force for as long as ORDR Processes Personal Data on behalf of Customer.

**14.3 Severability.** If any provision is held invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions remain in full force and effect.

## Annex II — Technical and Organisational Measures (TOMs)

ORDR maintains the following technical and organisational measures to protect Personal Data:

### II.A Access controls
- Role-Based Access Control (9 roles, 41 permissions, hierarchy 0–15)
- Multi-factor authentication available for all users; enforced for administrators
- Principle of least privilege; quarterly access reviews
- SSO via SAML 2.0 / OIDC available to Customer (Enterprise tier)

### II.B Authentication
- Passwords stored using bcrypt (cost factor 12)
- JWT tokens (HS256, 30-minute access / 7-day refresh)
- API keys bcrypt-hashed and tenant-scoped
- CSRF double-submit protection

### II.C Encryption
- TLS 1.3 for all data in transit
- AES-256 at rest for primary database and backups
- Per-tenant Fernet encryption for sensitive credentials (e.g., ERP connector secrets)
- Key rotation supported

### II.D Logging and monitoring
- All user actions logged to immutable audit table (WORM)
- Application performance monitoring via Sentry
- Anomaly detection on rate-limit exceedances and authentication failures
- Audit logs retained on the WORM ledger for 7 years; operational logs for 90 days

### II.E Data integrity
- WORM tables (NO UPDATE / NO DELETE) enforced at PostgreSQL layer
- SHA-256 hash chain per tenant, verified daily by automated cron
- Tampering detected within 24 hours

### II.F Network security
- Managed PaaS deployment on Render and Vercel
- No exposed administrative interfaces
- Backend ↔ database via private network (not internet-routed)
- Cloudflare DDoS protection
- Application-layer rate limiting (60 req/min per user/IP)

### II.G Backup and resilience
- Daily automated backups, encrypted at rest
- Point-in-time recovery within 7 days
- 30-day backup retention
- Quarterly restore tests
- Documented BC/DR plan; RTO 4 hours, RPO 1 hour

### II.H Personnel security
- Background checks for personnel with access to production systems
- Confidentiality obligations in employment / contractor agreements
- Annual security awareness training
- Access provisioning and deprovisioning tied to HR lifecycle

### II.I Vendor management
- Sub-processors required to have SOC 2 (or equivalent) and signed DPA
- Annual sub-processor review
- Public sub-processor list with 30-day change notice

### II.J Incident response
- Documented IR plan; 72-hour breach notification SLA
- Tabletop exercises quarterly; full drill annually
- Forensic capability via WORM logs and external forensics retainer

### II.K Compliance program
- Annual third-party penetration test
- OWASP ASVS Level 2 verified
- ISO 27001 controls mapped
- SOC 2 Type II audit in progress

### II.L Secure software development
- Code review required on all changes
- Static analysis via CodeQL
- Dependency scanning via Dependabot
- Branch protection and signed commits
