## Slide 3 — Why now

**Three forcing functions, stacked:**

1. **EMIR Refit (Apr 2024)** — new UTI generation + ISO 20022 mandates; existing vendors scrambling
2. **Post-FTX counterparty risk reckoning** — every CFO is being asked "what's our SVB?"
3. **AI skepticism in regulated workflows** — auditors increasingly hostile to opaque ML; deterministic systems are the safe bet

**Footer:** *"The platforms built five years ago for a different regime are now liabilities."*

**Speaker notes:**
> "Three independent regulatory and macro forces are converging on this exact product category. We started building before EMIR Refit — that's why we have working UTI-stamped XML in production today, and our competitors don't."

## Slide 4 — The wedge

**Headline:** Deterministic + auditable beats AI + opaque.

**Two-column comparison:**

| AI-driven optimization | ORDR's deterministic engine |
|---|---|
| "Why this hedge?" → confidence interval | "Why this hedge?" → cite the rule |
| Re-trains on data → not replayable | Same inputs → same outputs forever |
| Black-box for auditors | Every step signed + hashed |
| Fast to demo, slow to defend | Slow to demo, fast to defend |

**Footer:** *"Treasurers don't get fired for being slow. They get fired for failing audit."*

**Speaker notes:**
> "We deliberately don't ship ML in the engine. That's not a limitation — it's the product. The minute we add ML, we lose the auditor narrative that's our entire moat."

## Pitch tips

1. **Slide 4 (the wedge) is the slide.** If you only have one slide of attention, that's it. Practice it cold.
2. **Don't apologize for not having ML.** Frame it as deliberate. Investors who push back on this aren't your investor.
3. **The auditor angle wins more often than the treasurer angle** in fund partner meetings — they intuitively understand audit defensibility as a moat.
4. **Have a 3-minute version ready.** First-meeting pitches drift. The 3-minute version: market → why now → wedge → traction → ask.
